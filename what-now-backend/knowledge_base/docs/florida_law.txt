SECTION 1: OVERVIEW — FLORIDA AS A CHANGED LEGAL LANDSCAPE (2023 REFORM)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Florida historically was one of the most plaintiff-friendly states in America for
personal injury litigation. House Bill 837 (2023), signed by Governor Ron DeSantis
and effective March 24, 2023, fundamentally reshaped Florida tort law in favor of
defendants and insurers. Two changes matter most for every injured person:

CHANGE 1 — COMPARATIVE FAULT: Florida moved from PURE comparative negligence
(you could recover even if 99% at fault, reduced proportionally) to MODIFIED
comparative negligence with a 51% BAR. If a jury finds you MORE THAN 50% at fault,
you recover NOTHING — zero dollars — regardless of how severe your injuries are.

CHANGE 2 — STATUTE OF LIMITATIONS: Florida reduced the general negligence SOL
from FOUR YEARS to TWO YEARS. Claims that previously could be filed within four
years of injury must now be filed within two years. Missing this deadline is a
PERMANENT BAR — courts lose jurisdiction and insurers know it.

These reforms apply broadly to negligence actions filed on or after March 24, 2023.
Existing cases filed before that date may be governed by prior law depending on
accrual and filing dates — a transitional issue requiring attorney review.

Other HB 837 provisions affecting injury claims include:
- Modified bad-faith insurance claim standards (higher burden for insureds suing
  their own carrier for unreasonable denial/delay)
- Changes to how medical evidence is presented at trial (admissibility of certain
  billing and treatment records)
- Adjustments to how comparative fault is apportioned among defendants
- Reduction of one-way attorney fee provisions in certain insurance disputes

For voice assistant users: if the injury occurred before March 2023 but not yet
filed, or if the two-year window is approaching, URGENCY is critical. Florida is
no longer a "plenty of time" state for PI claims.

SECTION 2: COMPARATIVE FAULT — HB 837 AND THE 51% BAR
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STATUTE: Fla. Stat. 768.81 (as amended by HB 837, effective March 24, 2023)

RULE IN PLAIN LANGUAGE:
Florida now uses modified comparative negligence. Each party's fault percentage is
determined by the jury (or judge in bench trial). Your total damages are then
REDUCED by your percentage of fault. If your fault exceeds 50%, you are BARRED
from any recovery.

FORMULA:
Net Recovery = Total Damages × (100% − Your Fault %)
If Your Fault > 50% → Recovery = $0

WORKED EXAMPLES:

Example 1 — Clear liability slip-and-fall:
Total damages (medical + pain/suffering + lost wages): $200,000
Jury finds you 10% at fault (looking at phone briefly)
Recovery: $200,000 × 90% = $180,000

Example 2 — Disputed liability car accident:
Total damages: $500,000
Jury finds you 35% at fault (following too closely in chain reaction)
Recovery: $500,000 × 65% = $325,000

Example 3 — 51% bar triggered:
Total damages: $1,000,000 (catastrophic spinal injury)
Jury finds you 51% at fault (failed to yield, disputed facts)
Recovery: $0 — COMPLETE BAR despite million-dollar injuries

Example 4 — Exactly 50% fault:
Total damages: $100,000
Jury finds you 50% at fault
Recovery: $100,000 × 50% = $50,000
Note: At exactly 50%, you CAN still recover. The bar applies only when fault is
GREATER THAN 50% (i.e., 51% or more).

HOW INSURERS USE THE 51% BAR:
Florida adjusters now aggressively argue comparative fault in every claim:
- "You weren't watching where you were walking" (premises)
- "You stopped short" / "You were speeding" (auto)
- "You provoked the dog" / "You ignored warning signs" (dog bite)
- "You were wearing improper footwear" (slip-and-fall)
- "Pre-existing condition means your fault for not protecting yourself"

Recorded statements are weaponized to extract admissions supporting 51%+ fault.
Even innocent phrases like "I guess I should have been more careful" can destroy
a case under the new standard.

DEFENSE STRATEGY FOR CLAIMANTS:
- Never admit fault at the scene or to adjusters
- Document hazard, lack of warnings, and defendant's control of premises
- Obtain witness statements before memories fade
- Preserve video before deletion (spoliation letter within 24 hours)
- Do not give recorded statement without legal counsel in serious cases

MULTIPLE DEFENDANTS:
Under Fla. Stat. 768.81, fault is apportioned among all negligent parties.
A defendant who pays more than their share may seek contribution from other
at-fault parties. Joint and several liability rules were also modified by HB 837
for certain categories of defendants — consult current statute for economic vs.
noneconomic damages allocation among defendants.

SECTION 3: STATUTE OF LIMITATIONS — 2-YEAR DEADLINE (FORMERLY 4 YEARS)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STATUTE: Fla. Stat. 95.11(3)(a) — as amended effective March 24, 2023

GENERAL NEGLIGENCE / PERSONAL INJURY: 2 YEARS from date cause of action accrues.
Accrual is generally the DATE OF INJURY (accident date), not when you finish
treatment or receive a diagnosis.

PRIOR LAW: Before March 2023, Florida allowed 4 years for general negligence.
Many people still believe they have 4 years — THIS IS WRONG for new claims.
Insurers will not remind you of the deadline.

OTHER FLORIDA LIMITATIONS PERIODS (selected):
- Wrongful death: 2 years from date of death (Fla. Stat. 95.11(4)(d))
- Medical malpractice: 2 years from discovery or when should have been discovered,
  with outer limits — complex; consult Fla. Stat. 95.11(4)(b)
- Intentional torts (assault/battery): 4 years (Fla. Stat. 95.11(3)(f))
- Property damage: 4 years (Fla. Stat. 95.11(3)(p))
- Contract actions: 5 years written, 4 years oral
- Libel/slander: 2 years

TOLLING EXCEPTIONS (limited — do not rely without attorney):
- Minors: SOL may be tolled until age 18 for certain claims, then 2 years runs
- Mental incapacity: tolling under Fla. Stat. 95.051
- Defendant absent from state: possible tolling under Fla. Stat. 95.051(2)(a)
- Fraudulent concealment: rare, high burden
- Insurance negotiation does NOT toll SOL unless written agreement signed (extremely rare)

CONSEQUENCE OF MISSING SOL:
Filing even one day late = court dismisses case with prejudice = PERMANENT BAR.
No amount of injury severity, clear liability, or insurer bad faith revives a
time-barred claim in standard negligence actions.

PRACTICAL TIMELINE:
Injury date: January 15, 2025
Last day to file lawsuit: January 15, 2027 (watch for weekend/holiday rules)
Best practice: File or settle at least 90 days before deadline to avoid courthouse
filing errors, server problems, or defendant identification delays.

VOICE ASSISTANT ALERT PHRASE:
"If your Florida injury happened more than 18 months ago and you haven't filed a
lawsuit or settled, you are in the danger zone. Florida now has only a 2-year
statute of limitations for personal injury."

SECTION 4: NO-FAULT PIP — $10,000, 14-DAY RULE, EMC THRESHOLD
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STATUTE: Fla. Stat. 627.736, 627.737 (Personal Injury Protection)

Florida is a NO-FAULT auto insurance state. Every Florida registered vehicle must
carry Personal Injury Protection (PIP) with minimum limits of $10,000.

WHAT PIP COVERS (regardless of who caused the accident):
- 80% of reasonable and necessary medical expenses
- 60% of lost wages
- 100% of replacement services (e.g., household help)
- Death benefit up to $5,000 (separate from medical PIP)

WHO PIP PAYS:
Your OWN auto insurer pays PIP benefits — even if the other driver was 100% at
fault. This is the "no-fault" system: you look first to your own policy.

PIP follows the person in many scenarios:
- You driving your insured car: your PIP
- Passenger in insured Florida vehicle: that vehicle's PIP
- Pedestrian struck by Florida insured vehicle: driver's PIP covers pedestrian
- Out-of-state visitor: complex — may involve Florida resident's PIP or own state

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
THE 14-DAY RULE — CRITICAL — MOST COMMON FLORIDA PIP TRAP
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Fla. Stat. 627.736(1)(a): PIP medical benefits are payable ONLY for services
rendered within 14 DAYS after the motor vehicle accident.

If you do NOT receive initial medical treatment within 14 days of the crash,
you forfeit ALL PIP medical benefits — the entire $10,000 (or $2,500) is lost.

WHAT COUNTS AS "INITIAL SERVICES":
- Emergency room visit
- Urgent care visit
- Physician office visit
- Chiropractic evaluation
- Ambulance transport to medical facility
- Telemedicine visit with licensed provider (if documented properly)

WHAT DOES NOT SAVE PIP:
- Calling your insurer within 14 days (insurer notification is separate requirement)
- "Planning to go to the doctor"
- Home rest without medical evaluation
- Massage or non-licensed treatment without initial medical visit

INSURER TACTIC: GEICO, Progressive, and State Farm Florida adjusters routinely
deny PIP citing the 14-day rule. Document date of first treatment with timestamped
medical records. If day 13-14, go to ER or urgent care immediately.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
EMC THRESHOLD — $10,000 vs. $2,500 CAP
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Even if you treat within 14 days, FULL $10,000 PIP is available ONLY if a licensed
physician (MD or DO), dentist, physician assistant, or advanced registered nurse
practitioner determines you suffered an EMERGENCY MEDICAL CONDITION (EMC).

Without EMC certification: PIP medical benefits capped at $2,500.

EMC DEFINITION (Fla. Stat. 627.732(16)):
Medical condition manifesting itself by acute symptoms of sufficient severity
that absence of immediate medical attention could reasonably be expected to result
in serious jeopardy to patient health, serious impairment to bodily functions,
or serious dysfunction of any bodily organ or part.

PRACTICAL EMC EXAMPLES:
- Broken bone, laceration requiring sutures, head trauma with concussion symptoms
- Cervical strain with severe spasm documented on exam (physician discretion)
- Soft tissue only with mild symptoms: EMC may NOT be certified → $2,500 cap

Chiropractors alone CANNOT certify EMC under Florida law — physician-level provider
must document EMC for full PIP.

PIP EXCLUSIONS AND LIMITS:
- Massage therapy, acupuncture limited post-reform
- Non-emergency treatment after EMC determination follows fee schedule
- Deductible ($500 or $1,000) and co-pay (20% medical) reduce net payment
- Death within 90 days of accident: PIP may pay remaining benefits to estate

PIP CLAIM FILING:
Notify your insurer promptly (policy may require "as soon as practicable").
Complete Application for Benefits (form from insurer).
Insurer has 30 days to pay or deny each claim with explanation.
Disputes go to PIP mediation or court — attorney fees limited post-HB 837.

SECTION 5: MOTOR VEHICLE INJURY THRESHOLD — PAIN & SUFFERING LAWSUIT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STATUTE: Fla. Stat. 627.737

Because Florida is no-fault for auto, you generally CANNOT sue the at-fault driver
for pain and suffering, lost wages beyond PIP, or general damages UNLESS you meet
the "serious injury" / tort threshold:

You must prove ONE of the following:
1. Significant and permanent loss of an important bodily function
2. Permanent injury within a reasonable degree of medical probability
3. Significant and permanent scarring or disfigurement
4. Death

Without meeting threshold: limited to PIP benefits + property damage claim.
With threshold met: full tort claim against at-fault driver including pain and
suffering, full lost wages, future medical, loss of consortium.

Threshold requires medical expert testimony in most cases — not just your complaint
of ongoing pain. MRI showing herniation, surgical recommendation, or physician
affidavit of permanency commonly used.

Bodily Injury (BI) liability limits on at-fault driver's policy then cap recovery.
Florida minimum BI limits (as commonly cited): $10,000 per person / $20,000 per
accident — many drivers carry only minimums. UM/UIM on YOUR policy critical.

SECTION 6: PREMISES LIABILITY — GENERAL FRAMEWORK
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Business and property owners in Florida owe a duty of reasonable care to invitees
(persons invited onto property for business purposes — customers, clients, guests
of commercial establishments). Homeowners owe similar duties to social guests and
invitees; trespassers receive lesser duties.

ELEMENTS OF PREMISES NEGLIGENCE (general):
1. Duty — defendant owned, occupied, or controlled the property
2. Breach — failed to maintain safe conditions or warn of known hazards
3. Causation — hazardous condition caused the fall/injury
4. Damages — medical bills, pain/suffering, lost wages, etc.

NOTICE is central in Florida premises cases:
- ACTUAL NOTICE: owner/employee knew of hazard ( saw spill, knew broken step)
- CONSTRUCTIVE NOTICE: hazard existed long enough that owner should have discovered
  it through reasonable inspection and maintenance

KEY FLORIDA CASES (background):
- Owens v. Publix Super Markets: constructive notice can be inferred from hazard
  duration or recurring nature of similar incidents
- Post-2010 and post-2023 reforms narrowed "mode of operation" theories for
  transitory substances — see Section 7 for current statutory standard

TYPES OF PREMISES CASES:
- Slip-and-fall on wet floor, spilled liquid, tracked-in rain
- Trip-and-fall on uneven flooring, carpet defects, parking lot potholes
- Inadequate lighting in stairwells or parking garages
- Swimming pool drowning / inadequate fencing (Florida pool safety statutes)
- Negligent security (assault in parking lot with known crime history)
- Elevator/escalator malfunction
- Construction site hazards

DEFENSES PROPERTY OWNERS RAISE:
- Open and obvious danger (should have seen hazard)
- Comparative fault (51% bar arguments)
- Lack of notice (especially transitory substances under 768.0755)
- Assumption of risk
- Idempotent condition (minor defect not actionable)

SECTION 7: SLIP AND FALL ON TRANSITORY SUBSTANCES — FLA. STAT. 768.0755
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STATUTE: Fla. Stat. 768.0755 — Actions against persons or entities in business
of owning, operating, or managing business or retail establishment for damages
related to transitory foreign substance in business establishment.

This statute is THE defining law for grocery store, big-box, restaurant, and
retail slip-and-fall cases involving spills, liquids, and other transitory
(not permanently fixed) substances on floors.

THE STATUTORY REQUIREMENT — PLAINTIFF'S BURDEN:
In a slip-and-fall action arising from transitory foreign substance, the injured
person must PROVE that the business establishment had ACTUAL or CONSTRUCTIVE
knowledge of the dangerous condition and should have taken action to remedy it.

CONSTRUCTIVE KNOWLEDGE — plaintiff must prove ONE of two pathways:

PATHWAY A — TIME/DURATION:
The dangerous condition existed for such a length of time that, in the exercise of
ordinary care, the business establishment should have known of the condition.

Translation: The spill sat on the floor long enough that a reasonable store with
proper inspection procedures would have found and cleaned it. Plaintiff must
present evidence of duration — employee testimony, video showing spill present
for 30+ minutes, circumstantial evidence of foot traffic through spill.

PATHWAY B — REGULARITY/FORESEEABILITY:
The condition occurred with regularity and was therefore reasonably foreseeable.

Translation: Spills of this type happen routinely in this area (e.g., self-serve
drink station, produce mister, entrance on rainy days) and the store should have
anticipated and guarded against it with mats, inspections, or warnings.

WHAT 768.0755 ELIMINATED / LIMITED:
The "mode of operation" doctrine — which allowed inference of notice from the
very nature of how the business operates (e.g., "they sell liquids, spills happen")
— was substantially curtailed. Plaintiffs can no longer rely solely on mode of
operation; they must prove actual/constructive knowledge under the statutory test.

PROCEDURAL REQUIREMENT:
Plaintiff must prove notice as part of case in chief BEFORE resting — cannot wait
to respond only to defense arguments. Failure to meet burden = directed verdict
for defendant.

EVIDENCE TO PROVE 768.0755 ELEMENTS:
- Surveillance video (request preservation immediately — spoliation letter day 1)
- Incident reports showing prior spills at same location
- Employee statements ("I knew the floor was wet")
- Rain logs / customer traffic on wet days at entrance
- Inspection logs (or lack thereof — discovery)
- Photographs of spill pattern, size, tracking (shows duration)
- OSHA or health department prior violations

DEFENDANT EVIDENCE:
- "Clean as you go" policies and sweep logs every 30 minutes
- Warning cones immediately placed
- Spill occurred seconds before fall (no time for notice)
- Plaintiff caused spill (dropped own item)
- Open and obvious wet floor with visible cone

PUBLIX, WALMART, TARGET, WINN-DIXIE, ALDI FLORIDA:
These defendants fight 768.0755 cases aggressively. National counsel, video
preservation teams, and comparative fault at 51% bar are standard. Early attorney
involvement improves video preservation and discovery success rate dramatically.

SECTION 8: DOG BITE LAW — FLA. STAT. 767.04 STRICT LIABILITY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STATUTE: Fla. Stat. 767.04 — Dog owner liability for damages to persons bitten

Florida is a STRICT LIABILITY state for dog bites — one of the most victim-friendly
dog bite regimes in America. Unlike Texas "one-bite rule," Florida does NOT require
proof that the owner knew the dog was vicious or had bitten before.

THE RULE:
Dog owner is liable for any damage done by their dog to a person bitten, if the
person is bitten while on or in a public place, OR lawfully on or in private
property including the property of the dog owner.

"Lawfully" on private property includes:
- Invitees (mail carrier, delivery driver, guest invited by owner)
- Person performing duty imposed by law
- Person on property at owner's implied or express invitation

OWNER LIABILITY IS AUTOMATIC if:
- Bite occurred
- Victim was lawfully present
- No applicable defense applies

DEFENSES UNDER 767.04:

DEFENSE 1 — PROVOCATION / NEGLIGENCE OF VICTIM:
If victim's negligence contributed, damages reduced by victim's fault percentage
under comparative fault (51% bar applies). Owner must prove victim teased, tormented,
abused, or unreasonably provoked dog.

DEFENSE 2 — TRESPASSING WITH WARNINGS:
If victim trespassed on owner's property AND owner had displayed readable sign
"Bad Dog" or equivalent warning at least 2 inches high in prominent place on
premises — owner may avoid liability UNLESS victim is child under 6 years old.

THE "BAD DOG" SIGN DEFENSE — DETAILS:
- Sign must be easily readable
- Must warn of bad dog (not just "Beware of Dog" in all contexts — courts analyze)
- Applies to trespassers on private property, NOT to lawful invitees on business
- Does NOT protect owner if victim under age 6
- Does NOT apply if owner was negligent independent of sign (e.g., leash law violation)

DEFENSE 3 — COMPARATIVE FAULT:
Victim's percentage of fault reduces recovery. At 51%+ fault, recovery barred.

HOMEOWNERS INSURANCE:
Most dog bite claims paid through homeowner's or renter's liability coverage.
Breed exclusions common: Pit Bull, Rottweiler, Doberman, Akita, Chow, wolf-hybrid.
Some Florida insurers refuse coverage or cancel after bite claim.

LANDLORD LIABILITY:
Landlord may be liable if landlord knew or should have known tenant's dog was
dangerous and failed to act — separate from 767.04 strict owner liability.

LOCAL LEASH ORDINANCES:
Violation of county leash law supports negligence per se argument but 767.04
strict liability applies to owner regardless of prior knowledge.

REPORTING:
Report dog bite to county Animal Control immediately — creates official record,
documents vaccination status, may trigger dangerous dog investigation.

SETTLEMENT NOTE:
Dog bite cases often involve facial scarring (permanent injury threshold for auto
doesn't apply — this is premises/strict liability). Scarring on child or woman
typically commands higher settlement/verdict ranges.

SECTION 9: WORKERS COMPENSATION IN FLORIDA
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STATUTE: Fla. Stat. Ch. 440

Workers compensation is the EXCLUSIVE REMEDY against your employer for workplace
injuries in most cases — you cannot sue your employer for negligence if covered
by comp (exceptions: intentional harm, no coverage when required).

COVERAGE REQUIREMENTS:
- Construction industry: employer with 1+ employee must carry workers comp
- Non-construction: generally 4+ employees
- Agricultural: 6+ regular employees, 12+ seasonal

BENEFITS:
- Medical benefits: all authorized reasonable and necessary treatment
- Temporary total disability (TTD): ~66.67% of average weekly wage
- Temporary partial disability (TPD)
- Permanent impairment benefits based on Florida impairment rating schedule
- Death benefits to dependents

FILING DEADLINES:
Report injury to employer within 30 days (sooner is critical — insurers deny late
reporting). Petition for benefits with OWCP/JCC within 2 years of injury or 1 year
after last indemnity payment — complex tolling rules apply.

THIRD-PARTY CLAIMS:
If defective equipment, negligent driver, or property owner (not employer) caused
injury, you may have BOTH workers comp claim AND separate personal injury lawsuit
against third party. Comp carrier may assert lien on third-party recovery.

CONSTRUCTION / SUBCONTRACTOR ISSUES:
Florida construction sites often involve multiple employers — proper defendant
identification requires attorney analysis of who was statutory employer.

SECTION 10: GOVERNMENT / SOVEREIGN IMMUNITY CLAIMS — 3-YEAR FRAMEWORK
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STATUTE: Fla. Stat. 768.28 — Waivers of sovereign immunity in tort actions;
limitation on liability.

Suing Florida state agencies, counties, cities, school boards, or other government
entities requires navigating SOVEREIGN IMMUNITY — special rules beyond ordinary PI.

NOTICE AND FILING REQUIREMENTS:
Before filing lawsuit against state agency or instrumentality, claimant must
PRESENT WRITTEN NOTICE OF CLAIM to the agency and Department of Financial Services.
Agency has 6 months to investigate and respond (deny or offer settlement).
Lawsuit generally cannot be filed until claim denied or 6 months expire without action.

LIMITATION PERIOD — 3 YEARS:
Action against state or agency for negligence must be commenced within THREE YEARS
after the cause of action accrues (date of injury), subject to notice requirements.

This 3-year period is LONGER than the general 2-year PI SOL but requires EARLIER
administrative steps. Missing notice can bar claim even if within 3 years.

DAMAGE CAPS (768.28):
- $200,000 per person / $300,000 per incident against state or local government
  (unless legislature passes claim bill for excess — rare, slow process)
- No punitive damages against government entities
- Interest limited

LOCAL GOVERNMENT (cities, counties):
Also governed by 768.28 with similar notice requirements. Some charter counties
have additional pre-suit procedures. Always identify correct entity — suing wrong
government unit wastes time and may blow deadlines.

EXAMPLES OF GOVERNMENT DEFENDANTS:
- City bus (transit authority) pedestrian strike
- Slip on wet floor in county courthouse
- School district playground injury
- State highway maintenance pothole crash
- Police vehicle accident (emergency vehicle rules may apply)

PRACTICAL ADVICE:
Government claims in Florida are procedurally unforgiving. Consult attorney within
first 90 days of injury if any government entity may be liable. Do NOT wait until
year 2 of general SOL thinking you have time — notice requirements may have already
expired.

SECTION 11: SETTLEMENT RANGES AND VALUATION IN FLORIDA
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Settlement values vary by county (Miami-Dade vs. rural Panhandle), injury severity,
liability clarity, insurance limits, and post-2023 jury conservatism. Ranges below
are illustrative pre-suit / mediation ranges, not guarantees.

SLIP-AND-FALL (retail, clear liability, 768.0755 met):
- Minor soft tissue, 6-8 weeks treatment: $5,000 – $25,000
- Moderate injury, chiropractic + PT, 3-6 months: $25,000 – $75,000
- MRI positive disc bulge, no surgery: $50,000 – $150,000
- Surgery (lumbar fusion, rotator cuff): $100,000 – $350,000+
- Hip fracture elderly with complications: $150,000 – $500,000+

MOTOR VEHICLE (threshold met, clear liability):
- Soft tissue only: $8,000 – $30,000
- Herniated disc, injections: $40,000 – $120,000
- Surgery with permanent impairment: $100,000 – $400,000+
- Catastrophic TBI / spinal cord: policy limits pursuit ($100K-$1M+)

DOG BITE:
- Minor bite, no scar: $3,000 – $15,000
- Significant scarring, single surgery: $25,000 – $100,000
- Facial scar child, multiple procedures: $75,000 – $300,000+

FACTORS REDUCING FLORIDA SETTLEMENTS POST-HB 837:
- 51% comparative fault arguments
- Shorter SOL pressure (insurers delay then offer low at deadline)
- EMC/PIP disputes reducing documented medical specials
- Conservative jury pools in certain circuits

SETTLEMENT PROCESS:
1. Complete treatment to MMI (maximum medical improvement)
2. Demand letter with medical records, bills, lost wage proof
3. Negotiation with adjuster (30-90 days typical)
4. Mediation (court-ordered or voluntary)
5. If no resolution — file before SOL and litigate

Liens to resolve from settlement:
- Health insurance subrogation (ERISA plans aggressive in Florida)
- Medicare/Medicaid
- PIP carrier subrogation in some scenarios
- Workers comp lien on third-party recovery

SECTION 12: TOP FLORIDA INSURERS AND ADJUSTER TACTICS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STATE FARM — Largest FL auto market share. Hurricane claims experience applied to
injury documentation demands. Quick recorded statement. 51% bar arguments.

GEICO — Aggressive 14-day PIP denial. Fast lowball before MRI. Mass-market adjusters
with high caseloads.

PROGRESSIVE — Snapshot telematics used to argue driving behavior. Quick settlement
push before full diagnosis.

ALLSTATE — Colossus software standardized low offers. Pre-existing condition focus.
Independent Medical Exams early.

CITIZENS PROPERTY — Primarily property but commercial lines touch injury claims.

UNIVERSAL PROPERTY / FLORIDA PENINSULA — Property-focused; injury on insured premises.

PIP-specific carriers (Direct General, etc.) — Strict fee schedule enforcement.

TACTICS UNIVERSAL IN FLORIDA POST-2023:
- Delay until SOL pressure
- Recorded statement fishing for 51% fault
- 14-day PIP technical denials
- Full medical history HIPAA requests (10+ years)
- Surveillance in moderate+ value claims
- IME doctor selected by insurer disputing treatment necessity

SECTION 13: FLORIDA-SPECIFIC PITFALLS — WHAT NOT TO DO
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

DO NOT miss the 14-day PIP medical treatment window — most catastrophic preventable
loss in Florida auto claims.

DO NOT assume 4-year statute of limitations — it is 2 years for negligence.

DO NOT admit fault at scene, in incident report, or recorded statement — 51% bar.

DO NOT give recorded statement to at-fault insurer without counsel in serious cases.

DO NOT sign broad medical authorization or blanket release.

DO NOT post injury activities on social media — surveillance and social media review
standard in Florida.

DO NOT skip reporting to store manager in writing for premises cases — creates
documentation gap.

DO NOT ignore government notice requirements — 3-year limit useless without proper claim
presentation to agency.

DO NOT treat with only non-physician providers for EMC determination if full PIP needed.

DO NOT settle before MMI while still treating — forecloses future damages.

APPENDIX B: VOICE ASSISTANT QUICK-ANSWER SCRIPTS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Q: "How long do I have to sue in Florida?"
A: "Florida now has a 2-year statute of limitations for personal injury negligence
claims. That changed in March 2023 from 4 years. The clock usually starts on the
date you were injured."

Q: "What is the 51% bar?"
A: "Since March 2023, if you are found more than 50% at fault for your injury in
Florida, you recover nothing. At 50% or less, your award is reduced by your fault
percentage."

Q: "What is the Florida 14-day PIP rule?"
A: "After a Florida car accident, you must receive initial medical treatment within
14 days to get any PIP medical benefits from your auto insurance. Without an
emergency medical condition certified by a physician, benefits cap at $2,500
instead of $10,000."

Q: "Is Florida a one-bite rule state for dogs?"
A: "No. Florida has strict liability under Statute 767.04. The dog owner is liable
for a bite if you were lawfully on the property, even if the dog never bit anyone
before. A Bad Dog sign may protect the owner only against trespassers, not guests
or children under six."

Q: "How do I win a grocery store slip-and-fall in Florida?"
A: "Under Statute 768.0755, you must prove the store had actual or constructive
knowledge of the spill — either it existed long enough they should have found it,
or spills like that happened regularly and were foreseeable. You must prove this
before resting your case."
