SECTION 1: OVERVIEW — ILLINOIS AS A PLAINTIFF-FAVORABLE STATE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Illinois is consistently ranked among the most favorable states in America for
injured workers and personal injury plaintiffs. Key reasons:

1. Modified comparative fault with 51% bar (not as harsh as 50% bar states)
2. Premises Liability Act simplified landowner duties (no invitee/licensee trap)
3. Strict liability for dog bites without prior knowledge requirement
4. Strong workers compensation benefits and broad coverage mandates
5. Cook County (Chicago) juries historically award higher damages than national average
6. No damage caps on most personal injury verdicts (medical malpractice has caps)

Illinois is NOT a no-fault auto state — injured parties sue at-fault drivers directly
for full damages without tort threshold (unlike Florida or New York serious injury
threshold). PIP is not mandatory in Illinois; MedPay optional on auto policies.

Countervailing factors:
- 2-year SOL requires timely action
- 1-year government notice period is extremely short
- Insurance reform debates periodically threaten joint and several liability
- Cook County court congestion can delay trials 2-4 years

SECTION 2: COMPARATIVE FAULT — 735 ILCS 5/2-1116 (51% BAR)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STATUTE: 735 ILCS 5/2-1116 — Contribution among joint tortfeasors; recovery of
damages for personal injury or property damage; affirmative defense of contributory
negligence or assumption of risk.

RULE:
In all actions for damages arising from personal injury, property damage, or
wrongful death, a plaintiff's recovery is barred ONLY if plaintiff's contributory
negligence is MORE THAN 50% of the proximate cause of the injury or damage.

If plaintiff's fault is 50% or less, recovery is reduced by plaintiff's fault
percentage — but NOT barred.

FORMULA:
Net Recovery = Total Damages × (100% − Plaintiff Fault %)
If Plaintiff Fault > 50% → Recovery = $0

WORKED EXAMPLES:

Example 1 — Chicago slip-and-fall, wet floor without cone:
Total damages: $150,000
Jury: plaintiff 20% at fault (should have seen some wetness)
Recovery: $150,000 × 80% = $120,000

Example 2 — Auto accident, disputed light:
Total damages: $400,000
Jury: plaintiff 50% at fault (exactly half)
Recovery: $400,000 × 50% = $200,000 — still recoverable at exactly 50%

Example 3 — 51% bar:
Total damages: $800,000
Jury: plaintiff 51% at fault
Recovery: $0

MULTIPLE DEFENDANTS — JOINT AND SEVERAL LIABILITY:
735 ILCS 5/2-1117 and 2-1118 govern contribution among joint tortfeasors.
Under 2-1117, defendants generally liable only for their proportionate share of
noneconomic damages EXCEPT in specific circumstances (intentional harm, alcohol-
related, etc.). Economic damages apportionment follows separate rules.
This is complex litigation territory — attorney essential in multi-defendant cases.

AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE:
Comparative fault is an AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE — defendant must plead and prove it.
Plaintiff not required to disprove their own fault in complaint.

INSURER TACTICS IN ILLINOIS:
- "You weren't watching where you walked" (premises)
- "Sudden stop" in rear-end cases where plaintiff stopped short
- Pre-existing degenerative disc arguments as "you were already injured"
- Failure to mitigate (didn't treat consistently)
- Phone use / distraction arguments

Illinois juries in Cook County may be skeptical of aggressive fault arguments
against clearly injured plaintiffs — but 51% bar remains real risk in close cases.

SECTION 3: STATUTE OF LIMITATIONS — 2 YEARS GENERAL, 1 YEAR GOVERNMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

GENERAL PERSONAL INJURY — 2 YEARS:
STATUTE: 735 ILCS 5/13-202
Actions for damages for injury to person must be commenced within 2 years next
after the cause of action accrued.

Accrual: Generally date of injury (accident date). Discovery rule applies in limited
circumstances (medical malpractice, some latent injury — different statutes).

WRONGFUL DEATH — 2 YEARS:
735 ILCS 5/13-209 — within 2 years after death (with exceptions for criminal
proceedings tolling in some homicide cases).

PROPERTY DAMAGE — 5 YEARS:
735 ILCS 5/13-205 — 5 years for property damage (relevant in auto cases splitting
injury and property claims).

MEDICAL MALPRACTICE — COMPLEX:
735 ILCS 5/13-212 — generally 2 years from when patient knew or should have known
of injury, with 4-year outer limit from act/omission in most cases.

MINOR TOLLING:
735 ILCS 5/13-211 — disability tolling; minors under 18 may have SOL tolled until
18th birthday, then 2 years runs — DO NOT assume unlimited time for child injuries.

CONSEQUENCE OF MISSING SOL:
Permanent bar. Dismissal with prejudice. Insurers track SOL and often delay offers
until deadline pressure mounts.

GOVERNMENT CLAIMS — 1 YEAR NOTICE (CRITICAL)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STATUTE: 745 ILCS 10/8-101 (Local Governmental and Governmental Employees Tort
Immunity Act)

Before filing suit against a local public entity (city, village, county, park district,
school district, transit authority, etc.), claimant must FILE WRITTEN NOTICE with
the applicable local government or its attorney within ONE YEAR after the date of
injury or when cause of action accrued.

NOTICE MUST INCLUDE (745 ILCS 10/8-101(d)):
- Name and address of claimant
- Name and address of attorney if represented
- Date, time, location of incident
- Factual description of circumstances
- Names of witnesses known to claimant
- Nature of injury and damages claimed

Failure to file timely notice = COMPLETE BAR to lawsuit against local government
entity — even if general 2-year SOL has not expired.

STATE GOVERNMENT:
Court of Claims jurisdiction for state of Illinois — different procedures under
705 ILCS 505. Notice and filing rules distinct from local government Act.

EXAMPLES REQUIRING 1-YEAR NOTICE:
- Slip on CTA (Chicago Transit Authority) platform
- Pothole injury on City of Chicago street
- Fall in Cook County courthouse
- School district playground injury
- Park district swimming pool accident
- Hit by city snow plow or municipal vehicle

PRACTICAL RULE:
If ANY government entity might be liable, file notice within 6 months to be safe.
Consult attorney immediately — 1 year passes quickly.

SECTION 4: PREMISES LIABILITY ACT — 740 ILCS 130
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STATUTE: 740 ILCS 130/2 — Premises Liability Act

HISTORICAL CHANGE:
Before 1995, Illinois distinguished between INVITEES (business customers — highest
duty), LICENSEES (social guests — warn of known dangers), and TRESPASSERS (minimal
duty). This distinction confused juries and often defeated legitimate claims when
plaintiff misclassified as licensee.

CURRENT LAW — SINGLE STANDARD:
740 ILCS 130/2 abolished the distinction between invitees and licensees as a
matter of law. The duty owed to both is the same: reasonable care under the
circumstances regarding the condition of the premises or in maintaining the
premises to prevent unreasonable risk of harm.

Translation: Whether you slipped at Walmart or at a neighbor's barbecue, the
property possessor owes you reasonable care — not two different legal standards.

WHAT PLAINTIFF MUST STILL PROVE (negligence elements):
1. Defendant owed duty (owned, occupied, or controlled premises)
2. Defendant breached duty by act or omission
3. Breach caused injury
4. Damages

NOTICE remains essential in slip-and-fall cases:
- Natural accumulation of ice/snow: Illinois follows "unnatural accumulation"
  doctrine in many cases — property owner generally NOT liable for natural snow/ice
  unless created unnatural condition (drainage, plowing pile, refreeze from owner act)
- Defective stairs, broken handrails, inadequate lighting: defect-based theories
- Negligent maintenance: failure to inspect and repair known hazards

KEY ILLINOIS PREMISES CASES (background):
- Post-Premises Liability Act cases focus on reasonable care, not status labels
- Open and obvious doctrine: if hazard open and obvious, owner may owe no duty to
  protect against it — unless owner should anticipate harm anyway (distraction,
  necessity of encountering hazard)

TYPES OF ILLINOIS PREMISES CASES:
- Retail slip-and-fall (Target, Jewel-Osco, Mariano's, Costco)
- Apartment complex negligent maintenance (landlord liability)
- Parking lot potholes and inadequate lighting
- Elevator/escalator injuries in Chicago high-rises
- Construction site injuries (often overlap workers comp + third party)
- Negligent security (apartment assault, hotel parking)

DISCOVERY FOCUS:
- Sweep logs, inspection records
- Prior similar incidents at location (FOIA for public property)
- Video surveillance (spoliation letter day 1)
- Employee training records on hazard response

SECTION 5: CHICAGO ORDINANCE NEGLIGENCE PER SE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Negligence per se: violation of a safety statute or ordinance establishes breach
of duty as matter of law if the ordinance was designed to prevent the type of harm
that occurred and the victim is within the class of persons the ordinance protects.

Chicago Municipal Code contains hundreds of ordinances that create negligence per se
arguments in personal injury cases when violated.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
COMMON CHICAGO ORDINANCES IN PI CASES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

SNOW AND ICE REMOVAL — MCC 10-8-180:
Property owners and occupants must clear snow from sidewalks within specified time
after snowfall ends (commonly cited: 3 hours after snowfall ends between 7 AM and
7 PM, or by 10 AM if snow stopped overnight). Failure to remove snow/ice creating
 hazardous sidewalk conditions supports negligence per se for pedestrian slip-and-fall
 on Chicago sidewalks adjacent to commercial/residential property.

BUILDING CODE VIOLATIONS — Title 13 (Building Code):
- Defective stairways without required handrails
- Inadequate guardrails on elevated surfaces
- Non-compliant elevator maintenance
- Inadequate exit lighting in commercial buildings
- Violation of fire egress requirements

When city building inspector cited violation predating injury, plaintiff's case
strengthened dramatically.

LEASH LAW — MCC 7-12-420:
Dogs must be on leash not exceeding 6 feet when off owner's property. Violation
supports negligence per se in dog attack cases (in addition to 510 ILCS 5/16 strict
liability against owner).

TRAFFIC ORDINANCES:
Chicago traffic code mirrors state law in many areas; municipal violations in
accidents on city streets may support per se arguments where ordinance designed
to protect pedestrians/drivers.

RESTAURANT / FOOD ESTABLISHMENT — sanitation and safety codes:
Slip on greasy kitchen floor in Chicago restaurant — health code maintenance
requirements may establish per se breach.

CONSTRUCTION ORDINANCES:
Permit requirements, barricade standards, sidewalk protection during construction —
violations causing pedestrian injury = per se + ordinary negligence theories.

HOW TO USE NEGLigence PER SE:
1. Identify applicable ordinance section
2. Prove defendant violated ordinance (citation, inspection report, testimony)
3. Prove violation caused injury to person ordinance meant to protect
4. Breach element established — still must prove causation and damages

DEFENSES:
- Compliance shown by evidence
- Ordinance not safety-related (administrative violation only)
- Victim not in protected class
- Comparative fault still applies under 735 ILCS 5/2-1116

SECTION 6: DOG BITE LAW — 510 ILCS 5/16 STRICT LIABILITY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STATUTE: 510 ILCS 5/16 — Animal attacks or injuries

Illinois Animal Control Act imposes STRICT LIABILITY on dog owners (and persons
harboring or keeping the dog) for injuries caused by the dog attacking, attempting
to attack, or injuring any person peacefully conducting themselves and lawfully
positioned wherever they may be.

KEY FEATURES:

NO ONE-BITE RULE:
Owner liable even if dog never showed aggression before. No need to prove owner
knew dog was dangerous.

NO PROVOCATION DEFENSE IN STATUTE (limited):
Unlike some states, Illinois strict liability under 5/16 is broad. Provocation
may affect comparative fault apportionment under 735 ILCS 5/2-1116 but does not
eliminate strict liability entirely in most cases.

PEACEFULLY CONDUCTING THEMSELVES:
Victim must not be tormenting, abusing, or attacking the dog. Trespasser committing
crime may lose protection.

LAWFULLY POSITIONED:
Includes public sidewalks, parks, victim's own property, delivery person on owner's
property with implied invitation.

LIABILITY EXTENDS BEYOND BITE:
"Attacking, attempting to attack, or injuring" covers knock-down injuries even
without puncture wound.

OWNER vs. KEEPER:
Person keeping or harboring dog (not just registered owner) may be liable — relevant
for landlord/tenant situations.

HOMEOWNERS / RENTERS INSURANCE:
Claims typically paid through liability coverage. Breed restrictions apply.

CHICAGO-SPECIFIC:
Leash ordinance violation (MCC 7-12-420) adds negligence per se layer.
Chicago Animal Care and Control investigates bites — report immediately.

SETTLEMENT IMPACT:
Facial bites, pediatric cases, scarring, nerve damage command premium values in
Cook County. Strict liability simplifies liability phase — fight is over damages
and comparative fault only.

SECTION 7: WORKERS COMPENSATION — MANDATORY, 45-DAY REPORT, TOP 5 STATE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STATUTE: 820 ILCS 305/ (Illinois Workers' Compensation Act)

Illinois is widely ranked among the TOP 5 most employee-favorable workers
compensation states in America based on benefit levels, scope of coverage, and
judicial interpretation favoring injured workers at the Illinois Workers'
Compensation Commission (IWCC).

MANDATORY COVERAGE:
Nearly every employer in Illinois with employees must provide workers compensation
insurance (exceptions extremely limited — certain agricultural, domestic workers).
NO opt-out like Texas non-subscribers. Employer without insurance faces penalties,
personal liability, and criminal exposure.

EXCLUSIVE REMEDY:
Workers comp generally bars direct negligence lawsuit against employer for workplace
injury. Trade-off: no fault proof required — employee need not prove employer
negligence to receive benefits.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
45-DAY REPORTING RULE — CRITICAL
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

820 ILCS 305/6(c): Employee should notify employer of accidental injury within
45 DAYS of occurrence. Failure to report within 45 days may bar benefits unless
reasonable excuse and no prejudice to employer — but insurers DENY late reports
aggressively.

BEST PRACTICE: Report IN WRITING same day or within 24 hours.
- Complete employer's accident report form
- Keep copy with timestamp
- Email supervisor and HR simultaneously
- Describe ALL body parts injured, not just most painful

BENEFITS AVAILABLE:
- Medical expenses: all reasonable and necessary, no dollar cap
- Temporary Total Disability (TTD): 2/3 of average weekly wage while unable to work
- Temporary Partial Disability (TPD): when working light duty at reduced wages
- Permanent Partial Disability (PPD): scheduled injuries (hand, leg, etc.) + wage
  differential for serious cases
- Permanent Total Disability (PTD): lifetime benefits if completely unable to work
- Vocational rehabilitation
- Death benefits to dependents

PPD SCHEDULE — ILLINOIS ADVANTAGE:
Illinois uses detailed schedule for body parts (820 ILCS 305/8(e)) with weeks of
compensation assigned per amputation/loss. Non-schedule injuries (neck, back) use
whole person impairment rating — often generous compared to other states.

THIRD-PARTY OVERLAP:
Construction worker injured by subcontractor's negligence, delivery driver hit by
other vehicle, factory worker hurt by defective machine — workers comp from
employer PLUS personal injury suit against third party. Comp carrier holds lien on
third-party recovery (820 ILCS 305/5).

RETALIATION:
820 ILCS 305/6(h) — illegal to fire or discriminate for filing workers comp claim.
Hard to prove but powerful when documented.

FILING WITH IWCC:
Application for Adjustment of Claim within 3 years of injury or 2 years after last
comp payment — whichever is later (820 ILCS 305/6(d)). Do not confuse with 45-day
employer notice — both required.

ILLINOIS vs. OTHER STATES — WHY "TOP 5 FAVORABLE":
- Higher wage replacement rate (66.67% vs. lower in some states)
- Broader definition of compensable injuries (repetitive trauma recognized)
- Arbitrators historically worker-sympathetic in Chicago
- Strong penalties for uninsured employers
- No arbitrary caps on medical treatment like some reformed states

SECTION 8: MOTOR VEHICLE AND OTHER COMMON INJURY CLAIMS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

AUTO ACCIDENTS:
Illinois fault-based system. Sue at-fault driver for full damages: medical, lost
wages, pain and suffering, disfigurement, loss of normal life, loss of consortium.

Minimum liability limits (Illinois): $25,000 per person / $50,000 per accident BI.
UM/UIM mandatory offer — must reject in writing to waive.

Uninsured motorist claims common in Chicago metro — carry high UM limits.

HIT-AND-RUN:
UM coverage on your policy; police report required within reasonable time.

RIDESHARE (Uber/Lyft):
Period 1/2/3 coverage analysis — app status determines which policy applies.

PRODUCT LIABILITY:
Strict liability and negligence theories for defective products causing injury —
2-year SOL under 735 ILCS 5/13-202.

ASSAULT/BATTERY:
Intentional tort — 2-year SOL. Homeowners insurance often EXCLUDES intentional acts.
Pursue assailant assets and criminal restitution.

NURSING HOME ABUSE:
Health Care Arbitration Act may require arbitration — complex. 2-year SOL with
potential discovery tolling in abuse cases.

SECTION 9: GOVERNMENT ENTITY CLAIMS — NOTICE AND IMMUNITY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

LOCAL GOVERNMENT — 745 ILCS 10/:
- 1-year written notice (Section 8-101) — MANDATORY
- Immunity exceptions for proprietary vs. governmental functions
- Willful and wanton conduct removes some immunity shields
- Damage caps in some categories — consult current statute

CTA (Chicago Transit Authority):
Special entity — notice to CTA within 6 months for some claims under separate
statutory provisions (705 ILCS 25/) — EXTREMELY SHORT. CTA cases require immediate
attorney consultation.

CITY OF CHICKAGO:
Notice to City Clerk or Corporation Counsel. FOIA for prior complaints at location.

ILLINOIS STATE:
Court of Claims — 705 ILCS 505 — 1-year filing limitation for most claims against
State of Illinois.

POLICE MISCONDUCT:
Separate statutes (740 ILCS 22/ etc.) — not standard PI but overlap in injury cases.

SOVEREIGN IMMUNITY DEFENSES:
Government entity may claim immunity for discretionary policy decisions vs.
ministerial acts — litigation-intensive distinction.

SECTION 10: SETTLEMENT RANGES AND VALUATION IN ILLINOIS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Cook County and collar counties (DuPage, Lake, Will) typically yield higher settlements
than downstate rural counties. Ranges illustrative for pre-suit negotiation.

SLIP-AND-FALL (retail, liability clear, Chicago):
- Soft tissue, 2-3 months treatment: $8,000 – $30,000
- Moderate, PT 6 months: $30,000 – $90,000
- Herniated disc, injections: $75,000 – $200,000
- Surgery: $150,000 – $450,000+

AUTO ACCIDENT (Cook County, clear liability):
- Soft tissue: $10,000 – $35,000
- Disc injury with treatment: $50,000 – $175,000
- Surgery, permanent impairment: $125,000 – $500,000+

DOG BITE (facial scar, child):
- Minor scar: $15,000 – $50,000
- Significant facial scarring: $50,000 – $250,000+
- Multiple surgeries pediatric: $100,000 – $400,000+

WORKERS COMP (administrative, not settlement but reference):
- Shoulder rotator cuff PPD: 20-40 weeks × weekly rate
- Back fusion PPD + wage differential: can exceed $200,000 lifetime value

SETTLEMENT PROCESS:
1. MMI from treating physician
2. Demand package to adjuster
3. Negotiation (60-120 days common)
4. Mediation (mandatory in some Cook County circuits before trial)
5. Lawsuit filing before SOL if no resolution

LIENS:
- Group health subrogation (BCBS Illinois, etc.)
- Medicare/Medicaid
- Workers comp lien on third-party case

SECTION 12: ILLINOIS-SPECIFIC PITFALLS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

DO NOT miss 1-year government notice — most common catastrophic error in Chicago cases.

DO NOT wait 45 days to report workplace injury — report same day in writing.

DO NOT assume natural ice/snow case is automatic win — unnatural accumulation required.

DO NOT give recorded statement without counsel in serious injury cases.

DO NOT confuse abolished invitee/licensee distinction with "no duty" — reasonable
care standard still requires proving breach and notice.

DO NOT file in wrong court — Illinois circuit court for county where injury occurred
or defendant resides (735 ILCS 5/2-101 venue rules).

DO NOT ignore workers comp lien on third-party recovery — lien reduces net settlement.

SECTION 13: IMMEDIATE ACTION CHECKLIST — ILLINOIS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

SAME DAY:
□ Medical care — ER, urgent care, or physician
□ Photograph scene, hazard, injuries
□ Report to property manager / obtain incident report (retail)
□ Report workplace injury IN WRITING to employer (workers comp)
□ Police report if auto accident or dog bite
□ Witness contact information
□ Identify if CTA, City of Chicago, or any government entity involved

WITHIN 7 DAYS:
□ Spoliation letter preserving surveillance video
□ Free consultation with PI attorney (especially Cook County / government cases)
□ Report dog bite to Animal Control
□ Document lost wages

WITHIN 45 DAYS:
□ Workers comp employer report MUST be completed (if workplace injury)

WITHIN 1 YEAR:
□ Government notice of claim FILED if any public entity liable

WITHIN 2 YEARS:
□ Lawsuit filed or case settled (general PI SOL)

ONGOING:
□ Consistent medical treatment without gaps
□ Avoid social media activity inconsistent with injury claims
□ Preserve all medical bills and EOBs

APPENDIX B: VOICE ASSISTANT QUICK-ANSWER SCRIPTS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Q: "How long do I have to sue in Illinois?"
A: "Illinois has a 2-year statute of limitations for personal injury from the date
of injury. If a city, county, or other local government might be responsible, you
must file a written notice of claim within 1 year — often before you'd even think
about suing."

Q: "What is Illinois comparative fault?"
A: "Under 735 ILCS 5/2-1116, if you are more than 50% at fault, you recover nothing.
At 50% or less, your award is reduced by your fault percentage."

Q: "Does Illinois have the invitee licensee distinction?"
A: "No. The Premises Liability Act abolished that distinction. Property owners owe
reasonable care to invitees and licensees alike."

Q: "Is Illinois good for workers comp?"
A: "Illinois is consistently ranked among the top 5 most employee-favorable workers
comp states. Coverage is mandatory for nearly all employers. You must report your
injury to your employer within 45 days, in writing, and you can receive medical
coverage and two-thirds of your wages while unable to work."

Q: "Are dog owners liable in Illinois?"
A: "Yes. Under 510 ILCS 5/16, dog owners are strictly liable if their dog attacks
or injures someone who is peacefully and lawfully present — even if the dog never
bit anyone before."
